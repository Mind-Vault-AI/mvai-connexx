# Chapter 7: Constructing Your Trading Plan — The Complete Blueprint

## Why You Need a Written Plan

A trading plan is a written document that defines every aspect of your trading business: what you trade, when you trade, how you enter, how you exit, how much you risk, and what you do when things go wrong.

Without a written plan, you are not trading — you are making it up as you go. And making it up in real time, under financial and emotional pressure, with money on the line, produces predictably poor decisions.

A written plan does three things:

1. **It makes decisions before the pressure hits.** You decide your risk per trade on Sunday evening when you are calm, not on Tuesday morning when a trade is going against you.

2. **It creates accountability.** You can objectively assess whether you followed your plan. "Did I follow my rules?" has a binary answer when the rules are written down.

3. **It enables improvement.** You cannot optimize a process that is not documented. Your plan is a living document that evolves as you learn.

## The Complete Trading Plan Template

Below is a comprehensive trading plan template. Fill in every section. Be specific. Vague plans produce vague results.

---

### Section 1: Trading Identity and Goals

```
TRADING PLAN
Name: _________________________
Date created: __________________
Last updated: __________________

MY TRADING IDENTITY:
I am a [style: day trader / swing trader / position trader]
who trades [instruments: forex / stocks / crypto / futures]
using [methodology: technical / fundamental / hybrid] analysis.

MY EDGE:
I make money by [describe your specific edge in 1-2 sentences].
Example: "I identify high-probability pullback entries in
established trends on the 4-hour timeframe, entering at
dynamic support with a minimum 2.5:1 reward-to-risk ratio."

FINANCIAL GOALS:
Starting account size: EUR ___________
Monthly return target: ___% (realistic: 3-8% for active traders)
Annual return target: ___%
Maximum acceptable drawdown: ___%

NON-FINANCIAL GOALS:
Process score target: ___/25 average
Maximum consecutive rule violations: ___ (then take a break)
Weekly journal completion: 100%
```

### Section 2: Market and Instrument Rules

```
MARKETS I TRADE:
Primary: ______________________________
Secondary: ______________________________
I will NOT trade: ______________________________

INSTRUMENTS:
[List specific instruments, e.g., "EUR/USD, GBP/USD, USD/JPY"
or "S&P 500 stocks above $20 with >1M avg daily volume"]

TRADING HOURS:
I trade during: ___ to ___ (my time zone: ___)
I do NOT trade during:
- First 15 minutes of market open (high volatility noise)
- Major news releases (unless it is part of my strategy)
- When I am tired, emotional, or distracted
- [Other restrictions]

SESSIONS:
Maximum trades per day: ___
Maximum trades per week: ___
If I hit ___ consecutive losses, I stop for the day
If I lose ___% of my account in one day, I stop for the day
If I lose ___% of my account in one week, I reduce size by 50%
```

### Section 3: Strategy Rules

```
SETUP CRITERIA (ALL must be met to enter):

1. Trend: ____________________________________________
   (e.g., "Price above 50 EMA and 50 EMA sloping up")

2. Structure: ________________________________________
   (e.g., "Price has pulled back to a previous support/
   resistance level")

3. Trigger: __________________________________________
   (e.g., "Bullish engulfing candle or pin bar at the level")

4. Confirmation: _____________________________________
   (e.g., "RSI above 40 and not overbought")

5. Context: __________________________________________
   (e.g., "No major news in next 2 hours, not Friday
   afternoon")

ENTRY RULES:
- Entry type: [market / limit / stop]
- Entry price calculation: ____________________________
- Maximum slippage: ___ pips/points

EXIT RULES:
Stop loss:
- Placement method: [structure / ATR / fixed]
- Calculation: _______________________________________
- Hard stop in market: [ ] Always

Take profit:
- Target 1 (___% of position): ______________________
- Target 2 (___% of position): ______________________
- Trail remaining: ___________________________________

Time stop:
- Day trades: close at end of session if target not hit
- Swing trades: re-evaluate after ___ bars
```

### Section 4: Risk Management Rules

```
POSITION SIZING:
Risk per trade: ___% of account
Maximum risk calculation:
Account size x Risk % / Stop distance = Position size

CORRELATION RULES:
Maximum correlated positions: ___
Maximum total portfolio risk: ___%
If correlated, reduce each position to ___% risk

DAILY LIMITS:
Maximum daily loss: ___% of account (EUR ___)
Maximum daily trades: ___
After ___ consecutive losses: stop for the day

WEEKLY LIMITS:
Maximum weekly loss: ___% of account
If reached: reduce position sizes by 50% next week

MONTHLY LIMITS:
Maximum monthly drawdown: ___%
If reached: stop trading for remainder of month,
review entire system before resuming
```

### Section 5: Pre-Trade Checklist

```
Before EVERY trade, confirm:

[ ] Setup meets ALL criteria from Section 3
[ ] R:R is >= ___:1
[ ] Position size calculated using the formula
[ ] Stop loss placed at logical level
[ ] Risk amount <= ___% of account
[ ] Total portfolio risk <= ___%
[ ] No major news events in next ___ hours
[ ] I am calm and focused (emotional check)
[ ] This trade is in my plan — I am not forcing it
[ ] I have written the trade in my journal BEFORE entering
```

### Section 6: Daily Routine

```
PRE-MARKET (__ minutes before session):
1. Review overnight/pre-market price action
2. Mark key levels on watchlist instruments
3. Identify potential setups (do NOT enter yet)
4. Check economic calendar for news events
5. Complete pre-trade mental preparation (5-minute ritual)
6. Set alerts at key levels

DURING SESSION:
1. Monitor alerts — do not stare at screens
2. When alert triggers, assess setup against checklist
3. If all criteria met, execute. If not, skip.
4. Manage open positions per management rules
5. Log all trades in journal immediately

POST-SESSION (__ minutes after close):
1. Close all day trades (if applicable)
2. Review all trades taken today
3. Complete process scores for each trade
4. Note any rule violations
5. Identify one lesson from today
6. Prepare watchlist for tomorrow
```

### Section 7: Weekly Review Protocol

```
EVERY [day of week]:

QUANTITATIVE:
- Total trades, wins, losses
- Net P&L and R-multiple total
- Average R-winner vs average R-loser
- Process score average
- Rule compliance percentage

QUALITATIVE:
- Best trade (process) and why
- Worst trade (process) and why
- Emotional traps triggered this week
- Rules followed and rules broken

IMPROVEMENT:
- One specific improvement for next week
- How I will measure it
- Review previous week's improvement target: achieved? Y/N
```

### Section 8: Emergency Protocols

```
IF I HIT MY DAILY LOSS LIMIT:
1. Close all positions
2. Close my trading platform
3. Write in journal: what happened, what I felt
4. Do NOT re-open platform until next session
5. Next day: reduce position sizes by 50%

IF I HIT MY WEEKLY LOSS LIMIT:
1. Close all positions
2. Take the rest of the week off
3. Complete a full system review
4. Reduce position sizes by 50% for the following week
5. Only resume normal sizing after 2 consecutive green weeks

IF I HIT MY MONTHLY DRAWDOWN LIMIT:
1. Close all positions
2. Stop trading for the rest of the month
3. Conduct comprehensive review:
   - Is my strategy still valid in current market conditions?
   - Am I following my rules? (check journal)
   - Is my emotional state impacting my decisions?
   - Do I need to adjust any parameters?
4. Resume with 50% position sizes
5. Only return to full size after recovering 50% of drawdown

IF I EXPERIENCE A "BLACK SWAN" EVENT:
1. Do not panic-close positions — assess the situation
2. If stops were in place, they should have triggered
3. If stopped out at worse than expected (gap/slippage),
   document and review
4. Reduce all exposure by 50% until volatility normalizes
5. Do not try to "trade the event" unless it is part of
   your documented strategy
```

## Living With Your Plan

Your trading plan is not a document you write once and forget. It is a living system that you:

- **Follow daily:** Every trading decision is checked against the plan
- **Review weekly:** As part of your weekly review, note any areas where the plan is unclear or incomplete
- **Update monthly:** Based on your journal data and weekly reviews, refine the plan
- **Overhaul quarterly:** Step back and assess whether the overall strategy is working

The sign of a mature trader is not a perfect plan — it is a plan that has been revised many times based on real evidence from real trading.

---

## Chapter 7 Key Takeaways

- A written trading plan removes emotion from real-time decisions
- Include every aspect: identity, goals, instruments, strategy, risk, routines, and emergencies
- The pre-trade checklist prevents you from taking trades that do not meet your criteria
- Emergency protocols protect you during inevitable drawdowns
- The plan is a living document — review weekly, update monthly, overhaul quarterly
- Following a mediocre plan consistently beats not following an excellent plan
